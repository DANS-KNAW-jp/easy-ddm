Help - Deposit dataset - Required Elements

Creator

(Required, Repeatable)

Name of the entity (person or organisation) that bears primary responsibility for the contents of the dataset. In many cases, this will be the project leader. Click "+" to enter multiple creators.

Enter the complete name, including title or titles. The creator's role in the project can be entered in brackets following the name.

Example: Dr EH Janssen (Project Leader and Head Researcher)

For organisations, please include the name of the sub-unit (e.g. department).

Example: Institute for Prevention and Social Psychiatry, Erasmus University Rotterdam

Other parties that were involved in the creation of the dataset can be listed under contributor(s) on the "Recommended elements" screen.

Title

(Required, Repeatable)

Title of the dataset.

Complete title that provides an accurate description of the dataset, and preferably includes temporal and spatial references. The title should resemble the name by which the research project is known in publications as closely as possible. Dutch titles should be entered for research projects that are known primarily by their Dutch name; English titles should be entered for research projects that are known primarily by their English name.

Examples:

  • The Opium Society: Dividend transfers to shareholders in Patria, 1756-1794
  • Culturele Veranderingen in Nederland, 1989

Click "+" to enter multiple titles (e.g. sub-titles or translated titles). We recommend entering titles in both Dutch and English.

Description

(Required)

Concise description of the contents of the dataset. Describe the research objective, type of research, method of data collection and type of data. Please limit your entry to a single paragraph of text.

A detailed description of the dataset and individual files can be uploaded as a separate document using the upload dataset option at the bottom of the screen.

Date created

(Required, Repeatable)

When were the data collected? Please enter the information as specifically as possible.

Example: 2004-01-01 through 2004-06-30 [yyyy-mm-dd].

Click "+" to enter multiple collection dates (e.g. if the data were collected in May 2004, May 2006 and May 2007).

Note: Use the temporal coverage field on the "Recommended elements" screen to indicate the period to which the data refer.

Access Rights

(Required)

DANS advocates the Open access guidelines of the OECD. This means that DANS promotes the free availability of research data and publications (option Open access below). However, there may be justified reasons for not making research data freely available. This may be because they contain personal data. There may also be contractual obligations with third parties.
DANS therefore allows data to be made available under additional access conditions, described below. You can combine these with a temporary restriction on your research data, using the Date available field.

Option: Open access
Select this category if you wish to grant Open access on your research data. This means that any registered user has immediate and unrestricted access to your dataset via EASY. It is important to note here that you will always be able to track down by whom your research data were downloaded. Each time a dataset is downloaded from EASY, the user's details are recorded. This information is available to you and other registered users, via the Activity logs in EASY.

Option: Restricted: request permission
Select this category if you wish to restrict the access to your data. Your dataset will be made available only to persons and/or organizations after your permission. You will receive an e-mail from DANS with the name of the person who wishes to access your data. You can then grant or deny access.
Please use this option only when strictly necessary. You may consider using a temporary restriction (see Date available field) combined with option Open access.

Option: Other access
This is a special access category. It is only for datasets that will not be made available via EASY. Please select this category if your datasets are made available elsewhere (e.g. by you personally), but you still wish to archive them with DANS in order to preserve them for the long term. This also applies to datasets that contain personal data as referred to in the Personal Data Protection Act (Wet Bescherming Persoonsgegevens). A dataset contains personal data if the data themselves relate to specific living persons, but also if the dataset contains information that, in combination with other datasets for example, could be used to identify specific living persons. If you wish to use this access category, please consult DANS first in all cases via: Help / Support . A specific license agreement will have to be negotiated in this case.

More information
More about access rights Open access and Restricted: request permission can be found in the License Agreement

Date available

You can use this option to impose a temporary restriction, e.g. because of a thesis/publication that is about to be completed. The default date available is today, implying no temporary restriction. To impose a temporary restriction, change the date into a date in the future. Until that date specified by you, there is an embargo period, in which the data files will not be accessible by any user. After that date, your dataset becomes automatically available for registered EASY users, under the conditions you specified as Access rights. The embargo period (time between today and the date specified) cannot be longer than two years and cannot be extended.

Upload dataset

Upload the dataset files and associated documentation.

Files can be uploaded individually or as one or more zip files. Zip files have the advantage of reducing upload time and preserving the arrangement (directory structure) of the dataset.

Size of the dataset. Uploading datasets that exceed 100 MB can take quite some time. We recommend dividing large datasets into multiple zip files. For extremely large datasets, please contact DANS concerning other ways to submit the datasets: info@dans.knaw.nl

Documentation. The options for using a data file are largely determined by the available documentation. DANS therefore requests that you submit all documentation that is relevant for re-using the dataset. In most cases, the documentation consists of the following documents:

  • a copy of the questionnaire or measuring instrument
  • a description of the sources (including archival sources) and selection procedures that were used
  • a description of the variables or a codebook
  • a copy of the research report containing a description of the data-collection procedure
  • description of any recoding or construction of variables
  • description of the data-cleaning procedure

For a complete overview of relevant documentation and further instructions, please refer to Instructions (by discipline) on the first screen in the "Deposit" section.
